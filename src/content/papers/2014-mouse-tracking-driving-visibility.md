abstract: "Within the LabEx IMobS3 / PEVMA project, this work studies how humans process visual information under reduced visibility (night, fog, rain, glare) in rich driving environments where critical targets like pedestrians must be detected in time. Combining psychophysical experiments with computational modelling, the study tracks computer mouse movement as a proxy for visual attention, manipulating both stimulus saliency (bottom-up) and driving context (top-down) to evaluate how drivers handle uncertainty in degraded visual conditions."
